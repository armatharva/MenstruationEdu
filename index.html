Step-by-Step Instructions

Follow these simple steps for proper pad application

1

Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water

2

Remove the pad from its wrapper and unfold it completely

3

Peel off the backing strip to expose the adhesive

4

Center the pad in your underwear with the adhesive side down

5

Press down firmly to ensure the pad sticks properly

6

If the pad has wings, wrap them around the underwear

Remember:

It's completely normal to feel nervous the first few times. With practice, using a pad becomes quick and easy. Don't hesitate to ask a trusted adult for help if you need it!

Choosing the Right Product for You

For Beginners:

Start with disposable pads - they're easiest to learn with and widely available.

For Active Lifestyles:

Tampons or menstrual cups offer the most freedom for sports and swimming.

For Eco-Conscious:

Menstrual cups and period underwear are the most environmentally friendly options.

Menstrual Hygiene Best Practices

Essential hygiene tips to keep you healthy and comfortable during your period

Daily Care

  • Change your pad or tampon every 4-6 hours
  • Wash your hands before and after changing products
  • Use warm water and mild soap for cleaning
  • Always wipe from front to back
  • Shower or bathe daily during your period

Proper Disposal

  • Wrap used pads in toilet paper or their wrapper
  • Never flush pads or tampons down the toilet
  • Use designated disposal bins when available
  • Wash hands after disposing of products
  • Keep disposal bags for when bins aren't available

Clothing & Comfort

  • Wear breathable cotton underwear
  • Change underwear daily during your period
  • Avoid tight-fitting clothes during heavy flow days
  • Keep extra underwear and products with you
  • Wash period underwear in cold water

What to Avoid

  • Don't use scented products on your vulva
  • Avoid douching - it disrupts natural balance
  • Don't leave tampons in for more than 8 hours
  • Avoid using super absorbent products unnecessarily
  • Don't ignore unusual odors or symptoms

Why Menstrual Hygiene Matters

Understanding the importance of cleanliness during your menstrual cycle

Health Benefits

  • •Prevents bacterial and fungal infections
  • •Reduces risk of urinary tract infections (UTIs)
  • •Prevents toxic shock syndrome (TSS)
  • •Maintains healthy pH balance

Comfort & Confidence

  • •Eliminates unpleasant odors
  • •Prevents skin irritation and rashes
  • •Allows you to stay active and social
  • •Boosts overall well-being

Connection to Overall Health

Your menstrual health is closely linked to your overall well-being. Good hygiene habits during your period help:

  • • Maintain your body's natural defense systems
  • • Support your immune system
  • • Promote better sleep and mood
  • • Build healthy lifelong habits

Warning Signs to Watch For:

See a healthcare provider if you experience: severe pain, very heavy bleeding, strong or fishy odors, fever, or unusual discharge. These could be signs of infection or other conditions.

Understanding Your Reproductive System

Learn about the anatomy and science behind menstruation

Female Reproductive System

Organ Functions

Uterus

A pear-shaped organ where a baby can grow. The inner lining (endometrium) thickens each month and is shed during menstruation if pregnancy doesn't occur.

Ovaries

Two small organs that store eggs and produce hormones like estrogen and progesterone. They release one egg each month during ovulation.

Fallopian Tubes

Tubes that connect the ovaries to the uterus. The egg travels through these tubes after being released from the ovary.

Cervix

The narrow lower part of the uterus that connects to the vagina. It produces mucus that changes throughout your cycle.

Vagina

A muscular tube that connects the cervix to the outside of the body. This is where menstrual blood flows out.

The Menstrual Cycle

Understanding the phases of your monthly cycle

Menstruation

Days 1-5

The uterine lining sheds, resulting in menstrual bleeding. This is the first day of your cycle.

Follicular Phase

Days 1-13

Hormones signal the ovaries to prepare an egg for release. The uterine lining begins to thicken again.

Ovulation

Around Day 14

A mature egg is released from the ovary and travels down the fallopian tube.

Luteal Phase

Days 15-28

If the egg isn't fertilized, hormone levels drop and the cycle prepares to start over.

Important Notes:

  • Cycle length can vary from 21-35 days (28 days is average)
  • It's normal for cycles to be irregular when you first start
  • Every person's cycle is unique to them
  • Tracking your cycle can help you understand your patterns

Hormones and Your Cycle

How hormones control your menstrual cycle and affect how you feel

Estrogen

Builds up the uterine lining and is highest just before ovulation.

Effects: Increased energy, better mood, clearer skin

Progesterone

Maintains the uterine lining after ovulation. Drops if pregnancy doesn't occur.

Effects: Can cause bloating, breast tenderness, mood changes

FSH & LH

Brain hormones that signal the ovaries to prepare and release eggs.

Effects: Control the timing of your cycle

Managing Hormonal Changes:

  • Track your cycle to predict how you might feel
  • Eat nutritious foods and stay hydrated
  • Get enough sleep, especially during your period
  • Exercise regularly to help balance hormones
  • Practice stress management techniques
